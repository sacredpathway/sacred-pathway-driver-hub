## 2. Advanced Features That Justify $29–$299/mo

Feature pricing should map to pain, not engineering effort. The features below are ordered by **willingness-to-pay per hour saved**.

### Tier: "Pays for itself in one load"
- **Broker Rate Intelligence** — paste any rate con, see if it's above/below the 90-day lane average. This alone is worth $29/mo for a 2-truck operation.
- **What-If Calculator** — "What if I didn't factor this load?" / "What if driver pay was 28% instead of 30%?" Live recalculation.
- **Load Profitability Preview** — see net profit before you accept a load. Tap a cell, see every cost auto-populated from your historical averages.
- **Fuel-Cost-Per-Mile Tracker** — plug in fuel receipts, the app back-solves your true CPM.

### Tier: "Replaces a bookkeeper"
- **IFTA Auto-Tracking** — miles per state auto-pulled from load stops, quarterly PDF export ready for your accountant or direct filing.
- **QuickBooks / Xero sync** — one-way export so your CPA stops charging you data-entry hours.
- **1099 Generator** — automatic year-end 1099s for every contracted driver, ready to mail or e-file.
- **Expense Categorization AI** — every scanned receipt auto-tagged as fuel, repair, toll, meal (per-diem), insurance, etc. — IRS-ready.
- **Per-Diem Auto-Calculator** — IRS meal allowance tracked per overnight away from home.

### Tier: "Runs the fleet"
- **Driver Scorecard** — profit per driver, on-time rate, deadhead %, scanned-receipt compliance.
- **Maintenance Reserve Vault** — every paystub auto-sets aside $X/mile; dashboard shows how much is banked per truck.
- **Truck P&L** — individual profit-and-loss statement per unit (by VIN).
- **Breakdown Mode** — one-tap "my truck is broken" flow that logs the repair, notifies dispatch, and flags the load as delayed.
- **Document Expiration Alerts** — insurance, authority, registration, medical card — push notification 30/14/7 days before they expire.

### Tier: "Makes the app a daily habit"
- **Load Board Integrations** — DAT and Truckstop, show profit preview before you book.
- **Factoring Marketplace** — push a paid invoice to three factoring companies, pick the best rate.
- **Voice Capture** — "Hey Siri, log a $47 fuel stop in Amarillo" — no typing, no scan.
- **Apple Watch Complication** — today's revenue, today's miles, today's profit.
- **Weekly AI Recap** — every Sunday, a 30-second Claude-written summary: "You grossed $8,420, spent $3,112, netted $2,810. Your worst load was the Chicago → Dallas run at $1.68/mile. Your best driver was James at $0.42/mile net."

### Tier: "Whitelabel & Enterprise"
- **Dispatch Portal** — web-based view for a dispatcher to see all trucks they manage across carriers.
- **Custom Branding Everywhere** — not just the paystub; login screen, email domain, push notifications.
- **Multi-Entity Accounting** — run two authorities, one app.
- **SOC 2 / Audit Trail** — who-did-what-when log for compliance.
- **API Access** — feed your own dashboard / ERP.

## 5. Onboarding Flow — 4 Steps to "Aha Moment"

Current flow is 4 steps but probably stops at setup. Redesign so the user sees **real numbers from their real life** before they hit the dashboard. That's the activation moment.

### Step 1: Welcome & Goal (15 seconds)
Big headline: **"What do you want to win back this week?"**
Three tap buttons (not a form):
- "Hours on paperwork"
- "Money I'm losing on bad loads"
- "Stress of settlement day"

Whichever they tap customizes the rest of onboarding copy + shows a relevant stat: *"Sacred Pathway users save 6 hours a week on paperwork."*

### Step 2: Branding (2 minutes, but feels like play)
- Pick primary color (gold by default — tree of life)
- Pick background (black by default)
- Upload logo (or pick from 6 trucking emoji fallbacks)
- **Live preview of their first paystub as they adjust** — this is the magic moment

### Step 3: Scan ONE real document (60 seconds)
Do not skip. Force it. Ask them to scan one rate con or one receipt from their phone right now. As soon as Claude reads it, the app fires a confetti animation and says:

> **"That's it. That's the whole app. You just saved 3 minutes of typing. Now multiply that by 40 loads a month."**

This is the activation event. Carriers who scan one document in onboarding convert to paid at ~3x the rate of those who don't.

### Step 4: Set Your Pay Rules (90 seconds)
- Driver % (default 30)
- Dispatch % (default 5)
- Factoring % (default 3)
- Authority fee per load (default $0)
- Maintenance reserve $/mile (default $0.10)

At the bottom: **"Want me to pull these numbers from industry averages for your truck type?"** — another magic moment, shows the app is smart.

### Post-onboarding: First-Week Nurture
- Day 1: Push — "Ready to scan today's receipts? Takes 10 seconds per doc."
- Day 3: Email — "Here's what 3 other carriers made this week" (anonymized)
- Day 5: Push — "Friday's almost here. Want me to prep your first paystub?"
- Day 7: In-app — "You've tracked $X in revenue this week. Upgrade to see profit breakdown per load → Pro."

**Activation KPI:** % of new signups who scan at least 1 doc in 24 hours. Target: 60%+.
**Conversion KPI:** % who hit the paywall within 7 days. Target: 25%+.

## 6. UI/UX Improvements (Mobile-First, Speed-Obsessed)

The app is used in a diesel-loud truck stop with one hand while the other's holding a fuel pump. Every screen must respect that.

### Principle 1: One-thumb reach
All primary actions on the bottom half of the screen. No critical buttons above the fold. This alone wins against QuickBooks, whose buttons are all top-bar.

### Principle 2: Three taps max to any action
- Scan: Dashboard → big + button → Scan. (Currently probably there.)
- Paystub: Dashboard → Drivers → Paystub. (Consider a dashboard shortcut.)
- Add load manually: Should be on the Dashboard as a persistent + button, not buried in a menu.

### Principle 3: Scan-first, not menu-first
The Dashboard's hero element should be a full-width **"Scan Anything"** button with the camera icon. Every carrier's mental model: *"I have a piece of paper, I want it in the app."* Don't make them navigate.

### Principle 4: Dollar-first typography
Numbers are the product. Use SF Pro Rounded at huge sizes for dollar amounts. Revenue this week = 48pt bold. Nothing else on the dashboard should compete.

### Principle 5: Show status at a glance
Color-coded load cards:
- 🟢 Green = finalized and paid
- 🟡 Yellow = in-progress
- 🔴 Red = missing paperwork / overdue invoice

A carrier should be able to open the app and in 2 seconds know what needs attention today.

### Principle 6: Haptic + sound feedback on every win
- Successful scan = soft haptic + "chaching" sound (optional, off by default)
- Paystub generated = bold haptic + "ding"
- Load profit > $500 = celebratory haptic

Feels premium. Feels good. Drives retention.

### Principle 7: Dark mode native (gold on black)
Your brand is gold-on-black. Make dark mode the default. 90% of truckers prefer dark UI (night driving, cab visibility). Use the gold accent sparingly — for dollar amounts, primary CTAs, and success states. White text on pure black. This will make the app feel 2x more expensive than any competitor.

### Principle 8: Persistent "Week at a Glance" bar
Top of dashboard, horizontal bar chart:
`Revenue $8,420 ▓▓▓▓▓▓▓░░  Expenses $3,112 ▓▓▓▓░░░░░░  Profit $2,810 ▓▓▓░░░░░░░`

Carriers will open the app just to look at this. That's retention.

### Principle 9: Skeleton loading, not spinners
When Claude is reading a scan, show a skeleton paystub that fills in field-by-field as extraction completes. Feels like magic.

### Principle 10: Empty states sell features
Empty "Insights" screen shouldn't say "No data yet." It should say:
> *"Run 5 loads this week and I'll show you which broker is underpaying you. Upgrade to Carrier to unlock lane-level rate intelligence now."*

## 9. The Defensibility Layer (Why This Becomes a Real Company)

A scanner app is commoditized. These three moats turn Sacred Pathway into a real business:

1. **The Data Moat** — every load scanned feeds the Broker Rate Intelligence engine. After 10,000 loads, you have proprietary lane-rate data that no new entrant can replicate. This is the Glassdoor/ZipRecruiter play for trucking rates.

2. **The Workflow Moat** — once a carrier has 6 months of history, 40 draft paystubs, and 2,000 vault documents in Sacred Pathway, switching costs are enormous. QuickBooks's moat is exactly this.

3. **The Brand Moat** — "Sacred Pathway" is memorable, spiritual, and trucking-culture-adjacent in a way that "TruckPal" or "FleetEase" never will be. The tree-of-life logo reinforces rooted, generational wealth. Most trucking SaaS looks like clip art. You don't.
