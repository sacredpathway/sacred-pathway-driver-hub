# Sacred Pathway — Carrier DM Outreach Pack

**Goal:** 20 qualified carrier conversations → 5–8 beta users → 3+ testimonials.
**Your offer:** Free white-glove setup + lifetime founder's pricing ($29/mo, normally $49+). Limited to the first 100 carriers.

Don't pitch. Get on a 10-minute call. The product sells itself once they see the paystub generate.

---

## 1. Target carrier profile

You want operators who feel the pain daily:

- **Owner-operators running 1–5 trucks** — they do their own settlements and hate it
- **Small carrier fleets (6–25 trucks)** — the owner usually still touches payroll
- **Dispatch-heavy operators** — already juggling broker PDFs, rate cons, BOLs
- **Active on social** — if they post about loads, fuel prices, broker drama, they're reachable
- **Under 5 years in business** — more open to new tools than 20-year veterans

Skip: mega-carriers (100+ trucks — bureaucratic), pure drivers (not buyers), freight brokers (wrong ICP).

## 3. The first DM — three variants

Pick based on how you found them. Keep it under 4 sentences. **Do not send a wall of text.**

### Variant A — Cold, context-based (use when you can reference their post)
> Hey [first name] — saw your post in [group name] about [specific thing they said, e.g. "factoring taking 5% off every load"]. I built a tool that auto-generates driver settlements and profit-per-load from a photo of your rate con. Would you be down to test it? Free for early carriers + I'll set it up for you personally.

### Variant B — Warm, no specific trigger
> Hey [first name] — looks like you're running [X] trucks out of [state]. I'm the founder of Sacred Pathway — we just built an iOS app that scans rate cons, tracks load profit in real-time, and generates driver paystubs automatically. Looking for 10 early carriers to test it free before we launch publicly. You open to a quick look?

### Variant C — Referral / mutual connection
> Hey [first name] — [mutual name] mentioned you might be a good fit for something I built. It's a phone app that turns your broker rate cons into driver settlements in about 30 seconds. Free for the first 100 carriers who help us test. Worth a 10-minute call?

### Rules for all three
- **Use their first name.** If they go by a handle, use it.
- **Drop "I'm the founder."** That earns 2x the reply rate over "we" language from a no-name app.
- **End with a yes/no question**, not "let me know if you're interested."
- **Never paste a link in the first message.** Links get ignored and some platforms throttle link DMs. Get the conversation going first.

## 4. The 3-touch follow-up sequence

If they don't reply:

**Day 3 — soft bump**
> Hey, not trying to be pushy — just wanted to make sure this didn't get buried. No worries if it's not a fit right now, just wanted to close the loop.

**Day 7 — social proof**
> One more thing and I'll leave you alone 🙂 — just wrapped setup for [a carrier name / "a 4-truck operator in Texas"]. They said the paystub generator alone saved them 2 hrs/week. Want me to send a 60-sec screen recording so you can see it?

**Day 14 — break-up**
> All good if now's not the right time. Closing the loop on my end — if it ever comes up, I'm here. Good luck out there.

The break-up message is counterintuitive but it works. Roughly **1 in 5 non-responders will reply to this one** because it feels like a door closing.

Drop these in word-for-word. These are the 8 you'll hear most often.

We handle the pre-accounting chaos: scanning rate cons, calculating driver pay, pulling fuel receipts into the right load. Then you just export clean numbers into QuickBooks. Want me to show you in 5 min?

### "It's too expensive / I can't afford another subscription."

### "I don't trust AI with my paperwork."
> I hear that a lot. Two things: your documents stay on your phone — nothing gets shared with anyone. And the AI just reads — you approve every load before it hits your books. Nothing is auto-submitted. You're always in control.

### "I'm too busy to learn a new app."
> That's exactly why I built it. Setup takes 4 minutes. I'll get on a call and do it WITH you — your first load scanned, your first settlement generated, done. If it takes longer than 10 minutes start to finish, I buy you lunch.

### "My wife / dispatcher / bookkeeper handles that."
> Perfect — can I send her a 2-minute video? If she hates it, no hard feelings. If she loves it, you just gave her 3 hours back every week.

### "Send me more info and I'll think about it."
> Happy to. Before I send a 10-page doc you won't read, can I ask — is the blocker (a) not sure if it fits your setup, (b) timing, or (c) budget? I can send info that actually answers that instead of generic stuff.

### "How is this different from [Motive / KeepTruckin / Samsara]?"
> Those are ELDs — compliance and tracking. We're the money side: rate cons, loads, driver pay, profit per load. You keep your ELD. Sacred Pathway sits next to it and handles the financial paperwork.

### "I'll take a look when I get a chance."
> Sounds good. One favor — can I lock in your founder's pricing now even if you test it next month? Once those 100 spots fill I can't honor $29 anymore. Takes 30 seconds, no card on file, just reserves your seat.

## 6. Getting to the call

The DM is not where you close. The call is.

Once they reply "yes" / "tell me more" / "how does it work":

> Awesome. Easiest way is a 10-min screen share — I'll scan one of your real rate cons (or a sample) and show you the full flow. Does [day] at [time] or [day] at [time] work? I'll send a calendar invite.

Have **Calendly** or a free scheduler set up before you start outreach. Friction here kills conversions.

**On the call, don't present. Demo.**
1. Ask them to text you a rate con from a real load (or use a sample).
2. Scan it on your phone while screen-sharing.
3. Show the load auto-populated, profit calculated, settlement generated.
4. Generate and email the paystub PDF to them live.
5. Ask: "Would this save you time every week?"
6. If yes: "Want me to set up your account right now, lock in founder's pricing?"

## 8. Mistakes to avoid

- **Don't bulk-copy-paste on Facebook.** Meta throttles accounts that send identical DMs. Change at least 2 words per message.
- **Don't lead with the price.** Lead with the pain.
- **Don't pitch features.** Pitch outcomes ("paystub in 30 seconds", "know your profit before you finalize the load").
- **Don't argue.** If someone says no, say "totally understand, good luck out there" and move on. They'll remember you as the polite one when their current tool breaks.
- **Don't DM and forget.** Follow up. 80% of wins come from follow-ups 2–4.
