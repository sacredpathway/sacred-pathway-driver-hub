A bad pre-trip ends one of two ways. Either you find the problem in the yard at 5 a.m. and fix it. Or DOT finds it on the side of I-40 at 11 a.m. and writes you up.

This is the checklist we run every shift. It maps to FMCSA Part 396 (Vehicle Inspection, Repair, and Maintenance) and 49 CFR 392.7 (the federal standard that says you can't operate a truck unless it's been inspected). It's built from real roadside experience — not a CDL textbook.

If you want to print it, scroll to the bottom. We've laid it out so you can fold it once and keep it in your DOT logbook binder.

## What FMCSA actually requires

Under 49 CFR 392.7, before you operate a commercial vehicle, you must be satisfied that the following items are in working condition. The federal rule names them by component group:

- Service brakes (including trailer brake connections)
- Parking brake
- Steering mechanism
- Lighting devices and reflectors
- Tires
- Horn
- Windshield wipers
- Rear-vision mirrors
- Coupling devices
- Wheels and rims
- Emergency equipment (49 CFR 393.95)

Notice what's not in the federal rule: "spend exactly X minutes" or "follow these 12 specific steps." FMCSA says you must be **satisfied** the equipment works. That gives you flexibility — but it also means you're responsible.

What the **CDL test** asks you to do is more detailed because the testers want you to demonstrate that you know each component. What the **real world** asks you to do is whatever it takes to know your truck is safe to drive.

This checklist covers both.

## The Exterior Walk-Around (10 minutes)

Start at the driver's door. Walk clockwise. Don't skip the back.

### 1. Driver's Door & Step
- Door latches solidly. Step grates aren't broken or slick.
- Mud flaps present (where required by state law).

### 2. Engine Compartment
- Hood latched.
- Oil dipstick reads within normal range.
- Coolant level visible in overflow.
- Power steering reservoir level OK.
- Belts not glazed or cracked. Hoses not soft, bulging, or cracked.
- No fresh oil, coolant, or fuel leaks under the engine.
- Wiring harnesses secure, no bare wires.

### 3. Front Suspension & Steering
- Tie rods, drag link, ball joints — no visible play.
- U-bolts tight, no cracks at the leaf springs.
- Steering box bolted solid, no leaks.
- Shock absorbers not leaking, not detached.

### 4. Front Tires (steers)
- **Tread depth:** 4/32" minimum on steers (FMCSA 393.75(b))
- **PSI:** Should match sidewall + load chart — typically 110-120 PSI for most line-haul setups
- No cuts exposing cord or belt material
- No bulges, sidewall damage, or knots
- Valve stems intact, caps present
- Lug nuts present (count them), no rust streaks ("Christmas tree" pattern = loose lug)

### 5. Front Lights
- Headlights both work — low + high beam
- Turn signals (left and right, front)
- 4-way flashers
- ID lamps and clearance lamps on top of cab and at corners
- Reflectors not cracked or missing

### 6. Front Body & Mirrors
- Bumper not cracked or hanging
- Both mirrors solid, not loose, glass intact
- Windshield no cracks larger than 1/4" (FMCSA 393.60), wipers contact the glass
- Defroster works (run it for 30 seconds — listen for fan)

### 7. Side of Cab (driver side)
- Fuel cap tight, no leaks at the fill neck
- Fuel tank brackets not cracked
- Battery box secured, no exposed wires
- Air dryer purges cleanly during initial pressure build
- Catwalk grating intact

### 8. Drive Tires (driver-side duals)
- **Tread depth:** 2/32" minimum on non-steer tires (FMCSA 393.75(c))
- Inner and outer dually inflation matched within 5 PSI
- No mismatched tire diameters
- No debris stuck between duals (rocks chew through valve stems)
- Brake drum/disc not cracked, no leaking wheel seals
- Brake chamber not damaged, push rod free travel within spec (1" max for standard chambers per FMCSA 393.47)

### 9. Coupling Area
- Fifth wheel locking jaws around the kingpin (no daylight visible between trailer and fifth wheel plate)
- Release handle in locked position
- Safety latch engaged
- Air lines and electrical line connected, not chafed against the frame
- Mud flaps present behind drives

### 10. Trailer Front
- Landing gear up
- Glad hands secure, no air leaks audible
- Front trailer lights work (clearance, ID)
- Trailer registration + permits inside the document holder

### 11. Trailer Sides
- Tires (each side): tread depth 2/32" minimum, no flats or splits
- ABS sensors connected
- Mud flaps present
- Side lights and reflective tape intact
- No body damage that could fail in transit
- For dry van: doors latched, seal intact (note seal # on BOL)

### 12. Trailer Rear
- Tail lights work (running, brake, turn — each side)
- Reflective conspicuity tape intact (49 CFR 393.13)
- License plate light works
- Mud flaps present
- ICC bar / under-ride guard solid, not bent or missing
- License plate present, current, readable

### 13. Passenger Side (walk back forward)
Repeat steps 4-9 on the passenger side. Drives, brake chambers, fuel cap, tires, lights. Don't shortcut.

## The In-Cab Pre-Trip (5 minutes)

After the walk-around, climb in and run the in-cab portion.

### 14. Air System
- Build pressure to governor cutout (~125 PSI) — should take under 3 minutes from 85-100 PSI
- Once at full pressure: turn engine off, key on, foot off brake
- Watch the air gauge: pressure should not drop more than **2 PSI in 1 minute** (FMCSA 393.45)
- Pump brake pedal slowly — at about 60-80 PSI, low-air warning light + buzzer activate
- Continue pumping — at about 20-45 PSI, parking brake auto-engages (spring brake)
- Restart engine, rebuild to governor cutout

If any of those checkpoints fail, **do not roll**. Tag the truck and call dispatch.

### 15. Lights from Inside
- Headlights low + high
- Turn signals (have someone walk back, or check trailer reflection in your mirror)
- Brake lights
- 4-ways
- Dash gauges all light up at startup

### 16. Steering Play
- With engine running and air pressure built, turn steering wheel back and forth
- More than 2 inches of play (about 10° of free movement) at the steering wheel rim = fail (FMCSA 393.209)

### 17. Wipers, Defroster, Horn
- Run wipers low + high, washer fluid spray works
- Defrost airflow audible
- Horn: hit it once. Should be loud, clean tone.

### 18. Mirrors Adjusted
- West Coast / hood mirror angles
- Spot mirrors
- Driver and passenger side flat mirrors
- Cab interior mirror (where present)

### 19. Seat Belt
- Latches solidly. No cuts, no fraying. (49 CFR 393.93)

### 20. Emergency Equipment
- 3 reflective warning triangles (or 6 fusees) — FMCSA 393.95
- Working fire extinguisher (10 BC minimum — 5 BC for vehicles under 10,001 lbs GVWR)
- Spare electrical fuses (not required if your truck uses circuit breakers)
- Optional but recommended: hi-vis Class 2 vest, flashlight, gloves

### 21. Documents
Open your DOT logbook binder. Confirm:
- Current vehicle registration
- IRP cab card
- IFTA license
- Current medical certificate (medical card)
- Insurance card
- Bill of Lading for current load
- Permits (oversize, fuel, etc. if applicable)

## Common Failures DOT Looks For

After years of reading inspection reports, these are the ones that catch drivers consistently:

1. **Tread depth on steers** (4/32" — most drivers don't measure)
2. **Brake chamber push rod travel** out of spec (over 1" indicates worn brakes)
3. **Lamps inoperative** — single tail light or marker
4. **Tires with cuts exposing cord** (you can't see them without crouching)
5. **Air loss rate** over 2 PSI/minute (FMCSA 393.45)
6. **Securement** — load improperly secured (FMCSA Part 393 Subpart I)
7. **Mud flaps missing or torn** (state-specific but commonly cited)
8. **Steering play** over 2 inches at the wheel
9. **No working horn**
10. **Expired medical card** (cleanup item — but instant out-of-service)

## What to Do If You Find a Problem

Use the FMCSA framework:

- **Critical fail (immediate out-of-service):** No air pressure, brakes inoperative, steering excessive play, broken kingpin, fifth wheel not coupled, no working lights at night → don't move
- **Repair before the next trip:** Cracked windshield over 1/4", marker lamp out, marginal tire (3/32" on drive) → fix today
- **Document it:** Note any defect on a DVIR (Driver Vehicle Inspection Report, FMCSA 396.11) at the end of the shift

If you spot something during the shift that you didn't catch pre-trip, pull over and call dispatch. Continuing to drive a truck you know is unsafe is the difference between a fine and a CDL suspension.
